Example 5b

Vertex Form: \( a \neq 1 \)

Page 194
Problem Statement
Write \( y = -2x^2 + 8x - 3 \) in vertex form. Identify the vertex.
\( y = -2(x^2 - 4x) - 3 \)
Step 1 Group variable terms and factor out the leading coefficient (-2).
\( \left(\frac{-4}{2}\right)^2 = 4 \)
Step 2 Calculate the value to complete the square inside the parentheses.
\( y = -2(x^2 - 4x + \mathbf{4}) - 3 - \mathbf{(-2)(4)} \)
Step 3 Complete the square. Subtract the distributed value to keep the equation balanced.
\( y = -2(x - 2)^2 - 3 + 8 \)
Step 4 Factor the trinomial and simplify the balancing term.
\( y = -2(x - 2)^2 + 5 \)
Step 5 Simplify the final constants to reach Vertex Form.

Result

Vertex: \( (2, 5) \)
