Example 4

Equation with Imaginary Solutions

PAGE 193
Problem Statement
Solve \( x^2 + 8x + 22 = 0 \) by completing the square.
\( x^2 + 8x = -22 \)
Step 1 Rewrite so the left side is of the form \( x^2 + bx \).
\( \left(\frac{8}{2}\right)^2 = 16 \)
Step 2 Calculate the value needed to complete the square.
\( x^2 + 8x + \mathbf{16} = -22 + \mathbf{16} \)
Step 3 Add 16 to each side of the equation.
\( (x + 4)^2 = -6 \)
Step 4 Write the left side as a perfect square and simplify the right.
\( x + 4 = \pm\sqrt{-6} \)
Step 5 Apply the Square Root Property.
\( x + 4 = \pm i\sqrt{6} \)
Step 6 Simplify the imaginary root using \( \sqrt{-1} = i \).

Final Answer

\( x = -4 \pm i\sqrt{6} \)
