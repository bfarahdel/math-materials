Using Row 8 of Pascal's Triangle to solve binomial probability problems.

Example 1: Problem Statement

"Find the probability of 6 white lambs and 2 black lambs in a litter of 8 lambs."
We use the expansion of (w + b)⁸, where w = white and b = black.

Pascal's Triangle (Addition Logic Highlighted)

Step-by-Step Explanation

1. The full binomial expansion of (w+b)⁸ is:

(w + b)⁸ = 1w⁸ + 8w⁷b¹ + 28w⁶b² + 56w⁵b³ + 70w⁴b⁴ + 56w³b⁵ + 28w²b⁶ + 8w¹b⁷ + 1b⁸

2. According to Row 8, there are 28 ways to get the 6 white, 2 black combination.

3. Total possible outcomes (2⁸):

2⁸ = 256

4. The final probability:

28 / 256 ≈ 10.9%
