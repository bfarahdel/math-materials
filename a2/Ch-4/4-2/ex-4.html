Example 4

Determine a Single Term

Find the fifth term of $(y + z)^{11}$.
Step 1
Write the general formula for the binomial expansion:
$$(a + b)^n = \sum_{k=0}^{n} \frac{n!}{k!(n - k)!} a^{n - k}b^k$$
Applying this to $(y + z)^{11}$:
$$(y + z)^{11} = \sum_{k=0}^{11} \frac{11!}{k!(11 - k)!} y^{11 - k}z^k$$
Step 2
Determine the value of $k$ and substitute into the formula.
Critical Logic: The first term starts at $k=0$. Therefore, the $n^{th}$ term corresponds to $k = n - 1$.

For the fifth term, we use k = 4.
The equation for the 5th term is:
$$\text{Term}_5 = \frac{11!}{4!(11 - 4)!} y^{11 - 4}z^4$$
$$= 330y^7z^4$$
Calculation: $C(11, 4) = \frac{11 \cdot 10 \cdot 9 \cdot 8}{4 \cdot 3 \cdot 2 \cdot 1} = 330$
