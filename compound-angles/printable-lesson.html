Step-by-Step Solution: sin(105°)

Finding the exact value of sin(105°)

Step 1
Rewrite the angle
sin(105°) = sin(60° + 45°)
Step 2
Apply the Identity
sin(60°)cos(45°) + cos(60°)sin(45°)
Step 3
Substitute Exact Values
(√3/2)(√2/2) + (1/2)(√2/2)
Step 4
Final Simplification

Compound Angle Formulae

Sine
sin(A ± B) = sin A cos B ± cos A sin B
Cosine
cos(A ± B) = cos A cos B ∓ sin A sin B
Tangent
tan(A ± B) = (tan A ± tan B) / (1 ∓ tan A tan B)
⚠️ Note: Observe the sign reversal in the Cosine formula!

Example: Identifying Quadrants

Problem:

Given sin A = 4/5 (obtuse) and cos B = 5/13 (acute),
find sin(A + B).

Step 1
Apply Identity
sin(A + B) = sin A cos B + cos A sin B
Step 2
Find Missing Values
A is obtuse (Quadrant II) → cos A is negative
Using 3-4-5 triangle: cos A = -3/5
B is acute (Quadrant I) → sin B is positive
Using 5-12-13 triangle: sin B = 12/13
Step 3
Substitute & Solve
(4/5)(5/13) + (-3/5)(12/13) = 20/65 - 36/65 = -16/65

Solving Trigonometric Equations

Solve: sin(60° - x) = 2 sin x

Domain: 0° < x < 360°

Step 1
Expand
sin 60° cos x - cos 60° sin x = 2 sin x
Step 2
Substitute Exact Values
(√3/2) cos x - (1/2) sin x = 2 sin x
Step 3
Eliminate Fractions (×2)
√3 cos x - sin x = 4 sin x
Step 4
Group like terms
√3 cos x = 5 sin x
Step 5
Rearrange to find tan x
√3/5 = tan x
Step 6
Solve for x
Reference angle = tan⁻¹(√3/5) ≈ 19.1°
Since tan x is positive, x is in Quadrant I or III:
x = 19.1° or 199.1°

Solution: Question 4

Finding cos(x - 60°)

Step 1
Identify required parts
cos(x - 60°) = cos x cos 60° + sin x sin 60°
We have cos x = 4/5. We need sin x.
Step 2
Find sin x
Since 0° < x < 90°, x is in Quadrant I (all positive).
sin² x = 1 - (4/5)² = 1 - 16/25 = 9/25
sin x = √(9/25) = 3/5
Step 3
Substitute & Calculate
= (4/5)(1/2) + (3/5)(√3/2)
= 4/10 + (3√3)/10
Step 4
Final Value
(4 + 3√3)/10
